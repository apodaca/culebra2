Terms to Know
  • SWE (Snow Water Equivalent): The amount of liquid water contained within the snowpack. It represents the depth of water that would theoretically result if the entire snowpack melted instantaneously.
  • WY (Water Year): A 12-month period from October 1st to September 30th. Hydrologists use this timeframe because precipitation that falls in late autumn and winter contributes to the streamflow of the following spring and summer.
